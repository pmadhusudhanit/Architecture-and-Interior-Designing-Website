Art Deco

Art Deco style may have debuted in Paris, but its migration to Miami during the 1930s means that Florida’s seaside hotspot is now home to one of the world’s largest and most well-preserved collections of Art Deco architecture.Miami’s take on Art Deco (dubbed “Tropical Art Deco”) maintains the style’s signature devotion to straight lines and its geometric interpretation of nature but incorporates its own flair through the use of nautical elements and colorful pastels.





Cape Cod

Cape Cod architecture is one of American architecture's most popular and easily recognizable styles. Named after the Massachusetts coastal region, where it is the signature style, Cape Cod houses are ubiquitous in New England and can be found across the United States. Admired for its simple, timeless, clean-lined rectangular silhouette, steep roof, and central door, a Cape Cod house is what a child would draw if you asked them to draw a house.





FarmHouse

A farmhouse or country house in India can vary from traditional to modern homes, all of them exuding a rural essence in the lap of nature. Apart from being a second home, it holds huge potential for appreciation and, thus, acts as an excellent real estate investment. You also have the freedom to dispose of a part of your land when you need liquidity. Owing to this flexibility, you can manage your asset easily and have the luxury of selling it when you can get better returns on investment.





French Provincial

French provincial architecture also known as French Eclectic architecture include Manor houses or chateaux homes which were built by French aristocrats beginning in the 1600s. The homes are characterized by arched doorways and symmetrically placed elements. They are usually two stories tall with steep hipped roofs.Known for its stately, manor-like appearance, French Provincial architecture has been a popular building style since the 1600s and remains a popular pick today.
